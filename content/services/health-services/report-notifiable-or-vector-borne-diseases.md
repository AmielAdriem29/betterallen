# Report Notifiable or Vector-Borne Diseases - Municipality of Allen

Allen RHU investigates and manages notifiable infectious diseases (such as COVID-19 or measles) and vector-borne diseases (such as dengue).

---

## What You Need to Know

| Information               | Details                                                         |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Office                    | Rural Health Unit                                               |
| Who can apply             | All residents and reporting parties                             |
| Service type              | Government to Citizen                                           |
| Estimated processing time | Around 120 minutes per case workflow, plus continued monitoring |
| Fees                      | None                                                            |

---

## How to Report and Avail Services

### Step 1: Report the case to RHU immediately

- Provide patient details, symptoms, and available records.
- RHU verifies the reported case.

### Step 2: Support investigation and response

- RHU may conduct interviews, contact tracing, diagnostics, and field inspection.
- For dengue cases, teams may coordinate clean-up drives, larvicide, fogging, or misting.

### Step 3: Follow health instructions

- Patients and households receive guidance on isolation, monitoring, and prevention.
- RHU coordinates updates with provincial and national health agencies.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Valid ID (government ID, school ID, employment ID, or barangay certification)
- [ ] Medical records, lab results, and prescriptions (if available)

### Eligibility

- Open to all who need to report a suspected or confirmed case in Allen.
